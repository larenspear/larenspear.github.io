About Me

I am a first year master's student in computer science at Texas A&M University.

I graduated from the University of Texas at Austin with a degree in chemistry in 2021. I started programming in the computational materials lab as part of the Freshman Research Initiative program (FRI) under Dr. Juliana R. Duncan. I primarily worked with modeling bimetallic catalytic systems for use in hydrogen fuel cells.

As I went through my degree, I discovered that what I really liked about my research was the math behind it. What especially interested me was how calculations could be sped up through efficient algorithms, as well as what could be done to speed up calculations on supercomputers, like the TACC resources I had access to.
